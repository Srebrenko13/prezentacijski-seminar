These look like snowdrops, likely Galanthus nivalis. They’re small white flowers that bloom very early in the year, often appearing while winter is still ending.

Key features visible here:

White, drooping bell-shaped flowers
Green markings near the top of the petals
Narrow, grass-like leaves
Typical early-spring woodland/garden setting

Snowdrops are popular in temperate gardens because they’re among the first flowers to bloom after winter.
