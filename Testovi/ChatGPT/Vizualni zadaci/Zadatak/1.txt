You made a small mistake when differentiating the inner fraction.

The function is

[
f(x)=\arctan\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)
]

and you want the tangent at (x_0=2).

First compute the point:

[
f(2)=\arctan\left(\frac{3-2}{2-1}\right)=\arctan(1)=\frac{\pi}{4}
]

Now differentiate correctly.

Using the chain rule:

[
f'(x)=\frac{1}{1+\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)^2}\cdot
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'
]

For the quotient derivative:

[
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'
=============================

\frac{(-1)(x-1)-(3-x)(1)}{(x-1)^2}
]

Simplify the numerator:

[
-(x-1)-(3-x)=-x+1-3+x=-2
]

So

[
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'=\frac{-2}{(x-1)^2}
]

Hence

[
f'(x)=
\frac{1}{1+\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)^2}
\cdot
\frac{-2}{(x-1)^2}
]

Evaluate at (x=2):

[
f'(2)=\frac{1}{1+1^2}\cdot\frac{-2}{1^2}
=\frac12(-2)=-1
]

So the tangent line is

[
y-\frac{\pi}{4}=-1(x-2)
]

or

[
\boxed{y=-x+2+\frac{\pi}{4}}
]

The circled slope (-\frac29) is incorrect because the derivative of the fraction was computed with ((x+1)^2) instead of ((x-1)^2).
