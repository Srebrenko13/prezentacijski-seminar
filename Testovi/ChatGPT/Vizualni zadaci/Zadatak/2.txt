You are finding the tangent line to

[
f(x)=\arctan!\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)
]

at (x_0=2).

The mistake is in the derivative of the inner fraction. You wrote

[
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'
=============================

\frac{-1(x+1)-(3-x)}{(x+1)^2}
]

but the denominator should be ((x-1)^2), and the numerator simplifies differently.

Using the quotient rule correctly:

[
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'
=============================

\frac{(-1)(x-1)-(3-x)(1)}{(x-1)^2}
]

Simplify the numerator:

[
-(x-1)-(3-x)
============

# -x+1-3+x

-2
]

So

[
\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)'=\frac{-2}{(x-1)^2}
]

Now differentiate the whole function:

[
f'(x)=\frac{1}{1+\left(\frac{3-x}{x-1}\right)^2}\cdot \frac{-2}{(x-1)^2}
]

At (x=2):

[
\frac{3-2}{2-1}=1
]

so

[
f'(2)=\frac{1}{1+1^2}\cdot \frac{-2}{1^2}
=\frac12(-2)=-1
]

Also,

[
f(2)=\arctan(1)=\frac{\pi}{4}
]

Therefore the tangent line is

[
y-\frac{\pi}{4}=-1(x-2)
]

or

[
\boxed{y=-x+2+\frac{\pi}{4}}
]

The circled slope (-\frac29) is incorrect because of the quotient-rule error.
