That's a Tasmanian Devil (Sarcophilus harrisii)! Here are some key facts:

Marsupial — the world's largest carnivorous marsupial, native to Tasmania, Australia
Distinctive features visible here: jet-black fur, the small white chest patch, pink/red ears (which flush red when agitated), and the thick, tapered tail
Powerful jaws — pound for pound, one of the strongest bites of any mammal
Scavengers & hunters — they eat prey and carrion, consuming bones and all
Nocturnal by nature, though this one appears to be out in daylight
Conservation status: Endangered — severely threatened by a contagious facial tumor disease (DFTD) that has wiped out large portions of the wild population since the 1990s

The animal in the photo looks healthy and well-fed, with that characteristic stocky, low-slung posture. The reddish tinge around the ears and muzzle is completely normal — it's a sign of good circulation and can intensify with excitement or stress.
