That's a Tasmanian Devil (Sarcophilus harrisii)! Here are some fun facts:

World's largest carnivorous marsupial — since the extinction of the thylacine (Tasmanian tiger) in the 20th century
Native to Tasmania, the island state of Australia
That white chest patch is clearly visible in the photo — a common marking
The reddish/pink ears flush with blood when excited or stressed, which you can see here
Despite their stocky build, they're surprisingly fast and can travel up to 80 km in a night foraging for food
Famous for their incredibly powerful bite — the strongest of any mammal relative to body size — capable of crushing bone
They're scavengers primarily, feeding on carrion, though they also hunt small prey
Unfortunately listed as Endangered, largely due to Devil Facial Tumour Disease (DFTD), a rare contagious cancer that has devastated wild populations since the 1990s
Conservation programs, including a disease-resistant breeding initiative and a reintroduction to mainland Australia in 2020, are giving the species new hope! 🐾
