These are snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis) — one of the earliest spring flowers, often blooming while snow is still on the ground.
A few things to note about them:

Structure: Each flower has three large outer white tepals and three smaller inner ones, which have a distinctive green marking at the tip — visible here on the partially opened blooms.
Stage: These are still in bud/early opening, which is why the petals are still closed and drooping gracefully downward.
Habitat: They're growing among leaf litter and dry grass, a very typical setting — they naturalize well in woodland edges and gardens.
Significance: In Croatia and much of Central Europe, snowdrops (visibaba in Croatian) are a beloved symbol of winter's end, usually appearing in January–March.
