Those are snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis) — one of the earliest spring-blooming bulbs. A few things to note about them:

Appearance: The distinctive drooping white flowers have three outer petals and three smaller inner petals with green markings, attached to green ovary caps.
Season: They typically bloom January–March, often pushing through snow — hence the name.
Habitat: Native to Europe and the Middle East, widely naturalized in gardens and woodlands. Given you're in Croatia, these are very much at home there.
Significance: In many cultures they symbolize hope and the end of winter.
Toxicity: All parts of the plant are mildly toxic if ingested.
