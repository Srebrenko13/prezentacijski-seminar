These are snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis), one of the earliest spring-blooming bulbs. A few things to note about them:

Appearance: The pendant white flowers have three large outer tepals and three smaller inner ones marked with green — you can just barely see the green markings peeking through in this photo.
Blooming season: Late winter to early spring, often pushing through snow — hence the name.
Habitat: They naturalize well in woodland settings, which matches the leaf litter and dappled light in your photo.
Origin: Native to Europe and the Middle East, but widely naturalized across temperate regions.
Toxicity: All parts are mildly toxic if ingested, though they contain galantamine, a compound used in Alzheimer's treatments.

Given your location in Croatia, these are very much at home — Galanthus nivalis grows wild across much of the Balkans. A lovely early-spring find!
