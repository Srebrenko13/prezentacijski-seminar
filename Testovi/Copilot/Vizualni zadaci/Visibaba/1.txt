Those are snowdrops — one of the earliest spring bloomers and a quiet little rebellion against winter. They’re delicate, but they’re also tough enough to push through frozen soil, which is part of their charm.

🌱 What you’re seeing
Snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis) — small white flowers with a drooping, bell-like shape.

They usually appear late winter to very early spring, often while frost is still around.

The green markings on the inner petals are a classic identifier.

🌼 Why they matter
They’re symbols of renewal, hope, and the transition to spring.

In Croatia — including around Vrbovec — they’re a familiar sign that warmer days are coming.
