The person in image_a0a85e.png is Mahatma Gandhi, the iconic leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is world-renowned for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, which he called Satyagraha.

Here is a brief overview of his life and his lasting impact:

Core Philosophy: Satyagraha
Gandhi’s primary contribution to political thought was the concept of Satyagraha (insistence on truth). It wasn't just "passive resistance," but a proactive, moral force based on:

Ahimsa: Total non-violence in thought, word, and deed.

Self-reliance: Encouraging Indians to be economically independent, symbolized by the Charkha (spinning wheel).

Asceticism: As seen in image_a0a85e.png, Gandhi adopted a simple lifestyle, wearing a traditional dhoti and shawl made of hand-spun yarn to identify with the poor.

Major Milestones
South Africa (1893–1914): Gandhi first developed his nonviolent tactics while fighting for the civil rights of Indians living under racial discrimination in South Africa.

The Salt March (1930): To protest the British monopoly on salt, Gandhi led a 240-mile march to the Arabian Sea to make his own salt, a pivotal moment that drew global attention.

Quit India Movement (1942): During WWII, he launched this mass protest demanding an orderly British withdrawal from India.

Independence (1947): India gained independence, though Gandhi spent much of that time fasting and praying to stop the violent communal riots between Hindus and Muslims during the Partition.

Global Legacy
Gandhi's methods influenced major civil rights and freedom movements worldwide, serving as a direct inspiration for leaders such as:

Martin Luther King Jr. (U.S. Civil Rights Movement)

Nelson Mandela (Anti-apartheid movement in South Africa)

His birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated in India as Gandhi Jayanti and recognized internationally as the International Day of Non-Violence.
