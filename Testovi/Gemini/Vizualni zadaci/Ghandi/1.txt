The man featured in the file Ghandi.webp is Mahatma Gandhi, the preeminent leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule.

Gandhi is world-renowned for his philosophy of Satyagraha, which centers on resistance to tyranny through mass nonviolent civil disobedience. His approach not only led India to independence in 1947 but also inspired civil rights and freedom movements across the globe.

Core Philosophies
Ahimsa: The principle of non-violence toward all living things.

Satyagraha: "Truth force" or "soul force"—the practice of nonviolent resistance to seek social and political change.

Swaraj: Self-rule, focusing not just on political independence but also on individual and community self-reliance.

Major Historical Milestones
The Salt March (1930): A 24-day march to the Arabian Sea to protest the British salt tax, which became a pivotal moment in the struggle for independence.

Quit India Movement (1942): A mass civil disobedience campaign demanding an "orderly British withdrawal" from India.

The Fight Against Untouchability: Gandhi dedicated much of his life to reforming the Indian caste system and advocating for the rights of the "Dalits," whom he called Harijans (Children of God).

"In a gentle way, you can shake the world." — Mahatma Gandhi

Gandhi’s legacy remains a cornerstone of peace studies and political activism, demonstrating that the moral high ground and collective nonviolence can be more powerful than military force.
