The person shown in image_a0a8f5.png is Mahatma Gandhi (Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi), a towering figure of the 20th century who led India to independence from British rule. He is world-renowned for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, which he termed Satyagraha.Key Philosophies & LegacyGandhi's life was defined by several core principles that continue to influence global civil rights movements:Ahimsa (Non-violence): The practice of being harmless to self and others under every condition. He believed that non-violence was the weapon of the strong, not the weak.Satyagraha (Truth-force): A particular form of nonviolent resistance or civil resistance. It isn't just "passive resistance" but a determined search for truth through peaceful means.Swaraj (Self-rule): While it meant political independence for India, for Gandhi it also meant individual self-governance and moral mastery.Simplicity: As seen in image_a0a8f5.png, Gandhi famously wore a simple dhoti and shawl made of homespun cotton (khadi). This was both a sign of solidarity with the rural poor and a protest against British-made textiles.Major Historical MilestonesEventYearSignificanceSouth Africa Activism1893–1914Developed his philosophy of nonviolent protest while fighting for the rights of Indians in South Africa.Non-Cooperation Movement1920–1922Encouraged Indians to boycott British institutions, goods, and honors.The Salt March1930A 240-mile march to the sea to protest the British salt tax, sparking a massive civil disobedience movement.Quit India Movement1942A demand for an immediate British withdrawal from India during World War II.Indian Independence1947India achieved independence, though Gandhi was deeply saddened by the violent partition of India and Pakistan.Gandhi's approach inspired later leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. in the United States and Nelson Mandela in South Africa, proving that peaceful protest could dismantle powerful empires.
