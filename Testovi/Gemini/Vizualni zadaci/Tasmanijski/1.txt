Here is a breakdown of what makes this animal unique and some context regarding the image:

Key Characteristics
The World's Largest Carnivorous Marsupial: After the extinction of the Thylacine (Tasmanian Tiger), the devil took the top spot.

Distinctive Marking: You can clearly see the white chest stripe in Tasmanijski vrag.jpg. While most devils have this "necklace," about 16% of the population is solid black.

Famous Bite: For their size, they have one of the most powerful bites in the animal kingdom, capable of crushing bone.

The "Devil" Name: Early European settlers gave them this name because of their black fur, terrifying nocturnal screeches, and red-flushed ears (which you can see a hint of in the photo).

Ecological Importance
Tasmanian devils act as nature's "vacuum cleaners." They are primarily scavengers, eating carcasses that might otherwise spread disease.

Conservation Status: Endangered
The species is currently facing a massive threat from Devil Facial Tumor Disease (DFTD), a rare form of transmissible cancer.  Conservationists are working hard to create "insurance populations" on small islands and in fenced-off areas to ensure the species survives.
