The flowers in image_a1301a.jpg are Snowdrops (Galanthus). These are among the earliest bulbs to bloom, often pushing through the frozen ground or snow in late winter and early spring.Key Characteristics of SnowdropsAppearance: They feature three drooping, bell-shaped white petals (technically tepals) and three smaller inner petals often marked with green. ShutterstockIstražiteSymbolism: Because they bloom while winter is still lingering, they are widely recognized as symbols of hope, purity, and the coming of spring.Hardiness: They are incredibly resilient and can survive frost and light snowfall without damage.Tips for Growing ThemIf you're looking to add these to your own garden, here is what you need to know:FeatureRequirementLightFull sun to partial shade (they love being under deciduous trees).SoilWell-drained but moist; they don't like to dry out completely.Planting TimeLate summer or early autumn.NaturalizingThey spread over time, creating beautiful white "carpets" in the garden.A quick word of caution: while they are beautiful, all parts of the snowdrop plant are toxic if ingested by humans or pets, so it's best to enjoy them with your eyes rather than your taste buds!
